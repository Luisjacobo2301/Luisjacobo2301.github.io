Project Overview

Project CURIE-S (Composite Utilization for Rapid In-situ Engineering Solutions) is a pioneering initiative developed under the NASA L'SPACE program. Our team aims to address the challenges of manufacturing tools and components on the lunar surface during NASA's Artemis missions by developing an innovative 3D-printable composite material that combines lunar regolith with specialized resins.

As the Materials Science lead, my responsibilities include:

  • Conducting material property analysis and characterization
  • Developing and testing composite formulations
  • Optimizing the material for 3D printing applications
  • Collaborating with NASA mentors on technical specifications

Material Properties

  • Tensile Strength: 45 MPa
  • Thermal Stability: -150°C to 120°C
  • Print Resolution: 0.1mm
  • Regolith Content: 65% by volume
