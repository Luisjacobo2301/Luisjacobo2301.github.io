CURIE'S Project

May 2024 - Present NASA L'SPACE Program

Project Overview

With the NASA Artemis Program aiming to establish a sustained human presence on the moon, innovative methods for in-situ resource utilization (ISRU) are necessary to reduce the reliance on Earth-supplied materials. With the utilization of in-situ materials, NASA would alleviate some of the current challenges for lunar habitation, including payload weight, travel cost, and mission sustainability. The team’s project, CURIE-S, proposes the development of a novel 3D printing resin combined with the moon’s top fine layer of soil (regolith) capable of being printed in low gravity through the method of photopolymerization (curing). Extensive testing will address specific challenges with the Resin Regolith Mixture (RRM), such as the identification of the specific resin-to-regolith volume percent and the mechanical properties associated with it. This involves systematically varying the resin-to-regolith volume ratio between an optimal range and evaluating the mechanical properties of the various mixtures through tensile and compression testing, 3-point bending, and impact testing to obtain the ratio with the best attributes. The experimental mechanical property results will be compared with theoretical results from finite element analysis to ensure standardization of desired properties. After verifying experimental data, the project will advance to designing and prototyping a custom 3D printer capable of utilizing the RRM as a printing material in low gravity through digital light processing (DLP). Ultimately, the results of the project will have the potential to shape in-space additive manufacturing, providing astronauts with a way to create essential tools needed during long-term lunar missions.

Key Responsibilities:

  • Conducting material property analysis and characterization
  • Developing and testing composite formulations
  • Optimizing the material for 3D printing applications
  • Collaborating with NASA mentors on technical specifications
